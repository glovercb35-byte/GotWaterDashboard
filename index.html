Policy Implications

Georgia’s energy transition intersects directly with water availability. Expansion scenarios relying on gas and nuclear increase thermal water withdrawals, while renewable-heavy portfolios dramatically reduce strain on finite river basins. Sustainable planning requires structuring IRP pathways that reflect both load growth (including AI-driven data center expansion) and regional hydrological limits.
